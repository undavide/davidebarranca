  <p>
    This is the User Manual for VitaminBW 1.0, the Photoshop CC/CS6 script that spices up your black and white conversions!
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <!--more-->
  </p>
  
  <p>
    Vitamin BW is for sale on the <a title="Bigano e-Store" href="https://store.bigano.com" target="_blank">Bigano e-Store</a> and <a title="Vitamin BW on Adobe Exchange" href="http://bit.ly/17xRXkU" target="_blank">Adobe Exchange</a> for about € 26.00 / $ 34.95 (fluctuations due to the currency change) and by now is supported on Photoshop CC, CS6 (either Perpetual or Subscription), on both Mac and Windows.
  </p>
  
  <h2>
    Interface
  </h2>
  
  <p>
    Let&#8217;s have a look at the important features of VitaminBW &#8211; it&#8217;s a matter of selecting few options and start the processing.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2542" alt="Vitamin BW" src="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/VitaminBW.jpg" width="750" height="459" srcset="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/VitaminBW.jpg 750w, http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/VitaminBW-150x91.jpg 150w, http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/VitaminBW-300x183.jpg 300w" sizes="(max-width: 750px) 100vw, 750px" />
  </p>
  
  <div style="display: inline-block;">
    <p>
      <a href="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/VitaminBW_Conversion.jpg" target="_blank"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2538" alt="VitaminBW_Conversion_LOW" src="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/VitaminBW_Conversion_LOW-300x225.jpg" width="300" height="225" srcset="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/VitaminBW_Conversion_LOW-300x225.jpg 300w, http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/VitaminBW_Conversion_LOW-150x112.jpg 150w, http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/VitaminBW_Conversion_LOW.jpg 570w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>
    </p>
    
    <h2>
      1. Conversion
    </h2>
    
    <p>
      This is where VitaminBW applies its peculiar routine: you can select the <strong>Default</strong> conversion checkbox, or try the <strong>Boost</strong> option for a bit of extra punch.
    </p>
  </div>
  
  <div style="display: inline-block;">
    <p>
      <a href="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Filter_BIG.jpg" target="_blank"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2361" alt="Filter" src="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Filter-300x135.jpg" width="300" height="135" srcset="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Filter-300x135.jpg 300w, http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Filter-150x67.jpg 150w, http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Filter.jpg 568w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>
    </p>
    
    <h2>
      2. BW Filters
    </h2>
    
    <p>
      Simulating actual colored filters in BW Photography, to tweak the tonal rendering. See <a title="BW Filters conversion table" href="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/BWFilters_chart.png" target="_blank">here</a> for a conversion table.
    </p>
  </div>
  
  <div style="display: inline-block;">
    <p>
      <a href="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/VitaminBW_Tint.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2539" alt="VitaminBW_Tint_LOW" src="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/VitaminBW_Tint_LOW-300x225.jpg" width="300" height="225" srcset="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/VitaminBW_Tint_LOW-300x225.jpg 300w, http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/VitaminBW_Tint_LOW-150x112.jpg 150w, http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/VitaminBW_Tint_LOW.jpg 570w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a>
    </p>
    
    <h2>
      3. Tint
    </h2>
    
    <p>
      Besides the <strong>Neutral</strong> option, you can choose between <strong>Warm</strong>, <strong>Cool</strong> and <strong>Mixed</strong> (i.e. warm in the highlights and cool in the shadows).
    </p>
  </div>
  
  <h2>
    4. Preview
  </h2>
  
  <p>
    VitaminBW provides you with two preview images (Harbor, Woman) you can test the different Conversion, BW Filters and Tint combinations against. The Preview checkbox toggles the original color version.
  </p>
  
  <h2>
    5. Process &#8211; Single Tone
  </h2>
  
  <p>
    <img class="alignright size-full wp-image-2549" alt="SingleTone" src="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/SingleTone.png" width="326" height="379" srcset="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/SingleTone.png 326w, http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/SingleTone-150x174.png 150w, http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/SingleTone-258x300.png 258w" sizes="(max-width: 326px) 100vw, 326px" />The Single Tone button starts the processing &#8211; that is, the conversion to black and white according to the VitaminBW peculiar routine, BW Filtering and toning. As a result you get a <strong>Group</strong> in the Layers palette, containing from top to bottom:
  </p>
  
  <ul>
    <li>
      Tint layer
    </li>
    <li>
      Conversion layer
    </li>
    <li>
      Flat layer
    </li>
  </ul>
  
  <p>
    All these layers are kept to give you some extra control over the result (nonetheless, if you&#8217;re OK with it <strong>just flatten</strong> the Group and call quit). Let&#8217;s review how you can tweak them! Mind you, the following steps are totally <strong>optional</strong>.
  </p>
  
  <h3>
    Tint layer
  </h3>
  
  <p>
    This is what affects the toning (Neutral, Cool, Warm, Mixed). The layer is in <strong>Color</strong> blending mode, <strong>50% opacity</strong> so there&#8217;s room to either: saturate the result, raising the layer&#8217;s opacity; making it more subtle, lowering the layer&#8217;s opacity.
  </p>
  
  <h3>
    Conversion layer
  </h3>
  
  <p>
    Contains the peculiar VitaminBW conversion routine, and it&#8217;s provided with a blank layer mask. You can paint black in the layer mask to erase the contrast effect if needed &#8211; for instance in case the Boost option has introduced some unwanted halos.
  </p>
  
  <h3>
    Flat layer
  </h3>
  
  <p>
    This is a dull conversion, part of which is revealed only if you paint black the Conversion layer mask. Most of the time you can safely ignore it.
  </p>
  
  <h2>
    6. Triple Tone
  </h2>
  
  <p>
    If you&#8217;re inclined to experimentation try the Triple Tone, it&#8217;ll be fun!
  </p>
  
  <p>
    What this option does is to <strong>run three times the routine</strong>, applying the Conversion and Tint you&#8217;ve chosen, but selecting different BW Filters: one time <strong>Normal</strong>, one time <strong>Blue</strong>, one time <strong>Orange</strong>.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    As a result you end up with three VitaminBW layer groups &#8211; which different tonal rendering you can apply enabling their layer masks and painting in. The following illustration opens the first time you run Triple Tone.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    <img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2551" alt="TripleToneInstruction" src="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/TripleToneInstruction.png" width="570" height="458" srcset="http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/TripleToneInstruction.png 570w, http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/TripleToneInstruction-150x120.png 150w, http://localhost:8888/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/TripleToneInstruction-300x241.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 570px) 100vw, 570px" />
  </p>
  
  <p>
    True, you can always perform the same three-times routine manually with custom filters, but usually the Normal/Orange/Blue combination is very effective and it was worth implementing as a single button.
  </p>
